Meatloaf Recipe

Description

Enjoy this savory meatloaf, crafted with a blend of finely chopped vegetables, aromatic spices, and hearty bread crumbs. Each slice is tender and bursting with flavor, topped with a rich glaze that evokes a sense of home. The baking time will vary depending on the loaf’s dimensions and the type of pan you choose. Perfect for those crisp autumn or winter evenings, serve this dish alongside creamy mashed potatoes and a velvety mushroom gravy for a delicious meal

Meatloaf Ingredients

  • 1 medium carrot
  • ½ medium red bell pepper
  • 4 white mushrooms
  • ½ medium onion
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 2 ½ pounds ground chuck
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian herbs
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 cup plain bread crumbs
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon Sriracha sauce

Directions

  1. Gather all the ingredients.
  2. Preheat your oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C) and line a baking dish with lightly greased foil.
  3. In a food processor, finely chop the carrot, celery, onion, bell pepper, mushrooms, and garlic until almost puréed. Transfer this mixture to a large mixing bowl.
  4. Add the ground chuck, Worcestershire sauce, egg, Italian herbs, salt, black pepper, and cayenne to the bowl. Mix gently with a wooden spoon until just combined. Sprinkle in the bread crumbs and mix gently with your fingertips, being careful not to overmix.
  5. Shape the mixture into a loaf approximately 4 inches high and 6 inches across, and place it in the prepared baking pan.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for about 15 minutes, just until the meatloaf is hot.
  7. While the meatloaf is baking, mix brown sugar, ketchup, Dijon mustard, and Sriracha in a small bowl until the brown sugar dissolves to create a glaze.
  8. Remove the meatloaf from the oven and spoon the glaze over the top, letting a bit of it run down the sides.
  9. Return the meatloaf to the oven and bake for an additional 45 to 75 minutes, or until an instant-read thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the loaf reads at least 160 degrees F (70 degrees C). The exact cooking time will depend on the shape and thickness of the meatloaf.
