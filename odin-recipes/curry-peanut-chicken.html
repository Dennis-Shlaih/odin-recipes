Curry Peanut Chicken Recipe

Description

In this delightful dish, tender chicken is lightly coated with cornstarch and seared to form a delicate crust, ideal for absorbing a vibrant sauce made from spicy yellow curry paste, creamy peanut butter, and smooth mayo. Served over a bed of crisp marinated vegetables and fluffy white rice, it offers a perfect balance of bold and refreshing flavors.

Ingredients

  • 10 oz Boneless Chicken Breast Pieces
  • ½ cup Long Grain White Rice
  • 1 Tbsp Yellow Curry Paste
  • 6 oz Carrots
  • 2 Tbsps Mayonnaise
  • 1 Persian Cucumber
  • 1 Tbsp Mirin (Salted Cooking Wine)
  • ¼ cup Cornstarch
  • 3 Tbsps Roasted Peanuts
  • 1 Tbsp Apple Cider Vinegar
  • 3 Tbsps Sweet Chili Sauce
  • 2 Scallions
  • 1 Tbsp Smooth Peanut Butter Spread

Directions

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly to remove any impurities. Drain well.
  2. In a small pot, combine the rice, a pinch of salt, and 1 cup of water. Bring to a boil over high heat.
  3. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook for 13 to 15 minutes until the water is absorbed and the rice is tender.
  4. Turn off the heat, fluff the rice with a fork, and keep it covered to stay warm.
  5. While the rice cooks, wash and dry the fresh produce.
  6. Roughly chop the peanuts.
  7. Peel and grate the carrots using the large side of a box grater.
  8. Halve the cucumber lengthwise and thinly slice crosswise.
  9. Thinly slice the scallions, keeping the white bottoms and green tops separate.
  10. In a bowl, combine the grated carrots, sliced cucumber, vinegar, sweet chili sauce, and the white parts of the scallions.
  11. Season with salt and pepper, stir to combine, and let marinate for at least 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  12. In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the peanut butter spread, curry paste, mayonnaise, mirin, and 2 teaspoons of warm water.
  13. Pat the chicken dry with paper towels and place in a bowl.
  14. Season with salt and pepper, then add the cornstarch and toss to coat.
  15. Heat a thin layer of oil in a medium pan over medium-high heat.
  16. Once the oil is hot enough that a pinch of cornstarch sizzles immediately, add the coated chicken in an even layer, discarding any excess cornstarch.
  17. Cook without stirring for 3 to 4 minutes until lightly browned.
  18. Continue cooking, stirring frequently, for another 3 to 4 minutes until browned and cooked through. Turn off the heat.
  19. Discard any oil from the pan and transfer the cooked chicken to the bowl of sauce, stirring to coat. Taste and season with salt and pepper if desired.
  20. Serve the cooked rice topped with the marinated vegetables (including any liquid) and the dressed chicken. Garnish with the chopped peanuts and the green tops of the scallions. Enjoy!
